Japanese Porkbelly

This Japanese style porkbelly dish is one of my favorite recipes to break out on a special occaision. It is slightly involved, but the meat is so flavorful and fall appart tender. I used to make it by boiling it for 3-4 hours in broth, but have since learned that it can be done in about an hour with a pressure cooker. The leftover cooking liquid saves very well, and makes an excellent ramen base for the next day

Ingredients
All quantities listed are per portion
  • 1 lb porkbelly per person
  • 3 large cloves of garlic
  • Half of a shallot
  • 1 tbs soy sauce
  • 1 tsp honey
  • 1 tbs mirin
  • 1 quarter lemon
  • bone or vegitable broth (bouillon works
  • rice, ramen, or noodles to serve with
Steps
  1. Cube pork belly into 2 inch pieces
  2. Fry on all sides until crispy, Discard most of the fat when finished
  3. pork to instant pot along with a cup of broth. Cook on high pressure for 35 minutes
  4. remove most of the cooking liquid and switch to simmer with the lid off
  5. add garlic, shallots, and mirin
  6. Once the smell of wine has disipated, add soy sauce, honey, and lemon
  7. Reduce on high heat until sticky, adding more broth or soy sauce to taste
  8. Serve over rice (my prefered)
